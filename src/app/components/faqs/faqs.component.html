Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions
1. What is the process for adopting a pet?

The adoption process typically involves submitting an application, meeting the pet, completing an interview, and signing an adoption contract. Our staff will guide you through each step to ensure a good match between you and your new pet.

General Questions
2. What are your adoption fees?

Our adoption fees vary depending on the type and age of the animal.

General Questions
3. What is included in the adoption fee?

The adoption fee covers spaying/neutering, vaccinations, a microchip, and a health check.

General Questions
4. Can I adopt a pet if I live out of state?

Yes, but additional steps and requirements may apply. Please contact us for more details.

Before Adoption
5. Can I meet the pet before deciding to adopt?

Absolutely! We encourage potential adopters to spend time with the pet to ensure a good match.

Before Adoption
6. How do I know if a pet is right for me?

Consider your lifestyle, home environment, and the pet's needs. Our staff can help guide you in making this important decision.

Before Adoption
7. What should I bring when I come to adopt a pet?

Please bring a valid photo ID, proof of address, and any required adoption fees. If you rent, bring a copy of your lease or a letter from your landlord confirming pets are allowed.

Post-Adoption
8. What if my newly adopted pet has health issues?

We provide a post-adoption health guarantee. If your pet develops a health issue within the first 30 days, please contact us for assistance.

Post-Adoption
9. Can I return a pet if it doesn’t work out?

We understand that not all adoptions are successful. If you need to return a pet, please contact us to discuss the best options for the pet’s well-being.

Post-Adoption
10. Do you offer post-adoption support?

Yes, we offer post-adoption support including behavioral advice and resources to help you and your new pet adjust.

Specific Questions
11. Are all pets spayed/neutered before adoption?

Yes, all pets are spayed/neutered before they go to their new homes.

Specific Questions
12. Can I adopt a pet as a gift for someone else?

We recommend that the person who will be the primary caregiver be involved in the adoption process to ensure a good match.

Specific Questions
13. What if I have other pets at home?

We advise introducing new pets gradually. Our staff can provide tips and guidelines for a smooth introduction.
